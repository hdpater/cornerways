Isle of Purbeck

Explore Worth Matravers

Cornerways sits right on the South West Coast Path, in one of the best stretches of walking and running country in England — with world-class sea-cliff climbing a few minutes further along the same paths.

The main event

Walks & runs

This is what Worth Matravers does best. Quiet limestone lanes, ancient drove roads and a genuinely spectacular stretch of the Jurassic Coast all start from the village — most of it good for running as well as walking, with route options from a gentle half hour to a full day out.

Signature loop

Worth Matravers → Winspit → Dancing Ledge → Priest's Way

~5–6 mi2.5–3 hr walking~250m ascentModerate

Straight out of the village down to the eerie abandoned stone-quarry caves at Winspit, then along the coast path to the flat quarried shelf of Dancing Ledge — a favourite spot to swim in the old tidal pool. Loop back inland along the Priest's Way, the medieval path once walked by the parish priest on his way to Swanage.

Coast path

Dancing Ledge — Winspit — St Aldhelm's Head loop

~5.1 mi~2 hr 20~250m ascentModerate

A well-loved circular that strings together Dancing Ledge, Winspit and the dramatic headland at St Aldhelm's Head. A popular route for both hikers and trail runners, with birdlife along the cliffs for most of the year.

Route details →

Ancient path

The Priest's Way to Swanage

~4.75 km one wayEasyFlat / gentle

A quiet, largely traffic-free track between dry-stone walls, connecting Worth Matravers to Swanage. A good out-and-back run, or use it to make a longer coastal loop by returning along the cliffs.

Quarry & caves

Winspit & Seacombe loop from Langton Matravers

~9.5 km~2 hr 40Moderate

Takes in the old sea-cliff quarries at Seacombe and Winspit, both once central to the Purbeck stone trade and now atmospheric, half-wild places to explore — bring a torch if you want to poke into the quarry caves.

🧭 Further afield, still an easy drive

Durlston Country Park and Anvil Point (coastal path running with a proper cliff-top lighthouse), Old Harry Rocks near Studland, and the climb up to Corfe Castle for wide views over the whole Isle of Purbeck.

On the same cliffs

Climbing

The coast either side of Worth Matravers is one of the best-known sea-cliff climbing areas in the south of England — limestone quarries and cave systems turned into some genuinely excellent bolted and trad routes, right alongside the walking routes above.

Dancing Ledge

Sport & tradWell-bolted quarry + committing sea-level caves

One of the most important sport-climbing areas at Swanage, with a good range of grades and a spectacular setting — plus a sea pool for cooling off afterwards.

Winspit

Trad & sportOld quarry, adjacent caves

A short walk from Worth Matravers itself, Winspit's disused quarry faces are considered excellent for climbing, and — unlike some Swanage crags — aren't affected by the seasonal bird-nesting restrictions.

TradQuieter, more adventurous

🪢 New to sea-cliff climbing?

Local outdoor activity providers run guided climbing, abseiling and coasteering sessions at Dancing Ledge and Hedbury Quarry with all the safety gear provided — a good option if you don't have your own kit or experience with tidal sea cliffs.

Beyond the path

The rest of Purbeck

🏖️ Chapman's Pool

A secluded bay within easy reach of the cottage, popular with walkers and sea kayakers alike.

🏰 Corfe Castle

The romantic ruined castle and its village, about 4 miles away, with far-reaching views over the Isle of Purbeck.

🎡 Swanage

Under 6 miles east — a Victorian pier, sandy beach, and plenty of shops and cafés for a change of pace.

🍺 The Square and Compass

Worth Matravers' famously unspoilt village pub — pasties, local cider, and a small on-site museum of fossils.

🫖 Tea & Supper Room

By the village pond — a good stop for a cream tea before or after a walk.

🏘️ Langton Matravers

1.5 miles away, with further pubs, restaurants and the main parking for Dancing Ledge.
